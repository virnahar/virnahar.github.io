ITR JSON vs AIS JSON: what is the difference?

For Indian salaried taxpayers, both files matter. They are related, but they are not the same source of truth.

What ITR JSON represents

ITR JSON is your return data: income heads, deductions, tax liability, and values you (or your preparer) submitted while filing.

What AIS JSON represents

AIS JSON is an information statement from reporting entities. It can include salary, interest, dividends, securities transactions, rent, and TDS/TCS traces reported against your PAN.

Why mismatch happens

  • Different timing between transaction date, report date, and filing date.
  • Wrong mapping of AIS categories into ITR schedules.
  • Small interest/dividend entries missed in return preparation.
  • Name/PAN reporting errors by source institutions.

What to reconcile first

  1. TDS totals and major salary lines.
  2. Interest and dividend entries across all accounts.
  3. Capital gains and broker statement alignment.
  4. Any large amount that appears in AIS but not in ITR.
