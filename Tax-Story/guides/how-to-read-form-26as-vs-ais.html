How to read Form 26AS vs AIS

Both are important but they serve different purposes in your tax workflow.

Form 26AS in simple words

Form 26AS is primarily a tax credit statement. It helps you confirm credits like TDS/TCS and certain tax payments linked to your PAN.

AIS in simple words

AIS is a broader information statement. It may include income and transaction-level information beyond just tax credits.

What to check first

  1. Check major TDS entries and salary-related values.
  2. Review interest/dividend entries in AIS carefully.
  3. Compare high-value transactions with your supporting statements.
  4. Resolve large mismatches before filing or revising returns.

Use a reconciliation view

In Tax Story, load ITR + AIS together to detect mismatches quickly and avoid missing items that can trigger notices or confusion later.
